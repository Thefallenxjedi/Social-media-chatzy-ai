## 2. LinkedIn Post Copy
We recently looked at the metrics of a DTC cosmetics brand that migrated their cart recovery from email to WhatsApp.

The results are worth sharing:
* Email cart recovery open rate: 14%
* WhatsApp cart recovery open rate: 94%
* Conversion rate of sent recovery messages: Increased by 280%
* Extra monthly revenue recovered: $18,400

But the biggest lesson wasn't just the open rate. It was how the customers interacted with the recovery channel. 

Under the old email system, a customer who received an abandoned cart email had no easy way to ask a question. They would have to reply to a support email address, open a ticket, and wait 12 to 24 hours. The intent was dead. 

On WhatsApp, 34% of customers who opened the message replied. 

They asked questions like:
* *"Is this shade suitable for dry skin?"*
* *"Can I change my shipping address?"*
* *"Do you accept cash on delivery?"*

Because they were chatting in a low-friction channel, they felt comfortable asking the simple questions that were holding them back from purchasing. 

And because they had an AI agent answering these questions instantly, they checked out within 3 minutes of replying. 

The takeaway: 
Cart recovery isn't a broadcasting problem. It's a conversation problem. 

If you make it easy for your customer to ask, they will make it easy for you to sell.
